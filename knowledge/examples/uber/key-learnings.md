# Key Learnings — Uber

> Sources: Uber earnings calls 2023–2024, shareholder letters, public disclosures

## Recent Experiment Results

- **Uber One membership (2022–2024):** Grew from launch to 30M+ members. Key finding: Uber One members use both Mobility and Delivery, spend 4x more, and have 50% lower churn than non-members. The subscription model transforms Uber from transactional to habitual. This is the single most important growth lever.
- **Advertising business (2022–2024):** Scaled from $0 to $1B+ annualized revenue in ~2 years. Sponsored restaurant listings on Uber Eats have high merchant ROI. Post-checkout ads and in-car tablet ads (Journey Ads) are new high-margin surfaces. Learning: the marketplace data makes targeting highly effective — merchants who advertise see 30%+ incremental orders.
- **AV partnerships (2023–2024):** Waymo rides on the Uber app in Phoenix and SF showed strong consumer demand. Learning: consumers don't care whether a human or robot drives — they care about reliability, price, and wait time. Uber's role as the demand aggregator is validated. AVs likely augment, not replace, the human driver network in the medium term.

## Focus Shifts

- **From growth-at-all-costs to profitable growth:** Under CEO Dara Khosrowshahi, Uber pivoted hard to profitability starting 2022. First GAAP profitable quarter in Q2 2023. FY2024 Adjusted EBITDA of $6.5B. The growth years of subsidized rides are over.
- **From building AV tech to partnering:** Sold the Advanced Technologies Group (ATG) to Aurora in 2020. Now partners with Waymo, Cruise, and others. Learning: Uber's advantage is demand and the marketplace, not building self-driving cars.
- **From Mobility-first to multi-product:** Delivery is now almost equal in gross bookings to Mobility. The combined platform and Uber One are the growth strategy, not ride-hailing alone.

## What We Know Now

- Cross-platform usage (Mobility + Delivery) is the strongest predictor of long-term retention and LTV
- Uber One is a pricing and retention tool, not a revenue center — it drives more trips per user and cross-platform adoption
- Advertising is the highest-margin revenue stream and has a long runway — Uber's ad load and monetization are still far below peers like Google and Amazon
- Driver supply is the #1 constraint in most markets — driver earnings, experience, and retention are as important as consumer-facing features
- Regulatory outcomes (gig worker classification) can swing profitability by hundreds of millions — Uber's government affairs and legal teams are core to the business strategy
