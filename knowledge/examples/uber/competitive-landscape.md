# Competitive Landscape — Uber

> Sources: Uber 2024 10-K, public industry analysis, earnings call transcripts

## Key Competitors

1. **Lyft (Mobility — US/Canada)** — Uber's primary ride-hail competitor in North America. ~32% US rideshare market share vs. Uber's ~68%. Strength: simpler brand positioning, driver-friendly reputation. Weakness: no delivery/food platform, limited international presence, smaller network.
2. **DoorDash (Delivery — US)** — #1 US food delivery by market share (~67% vs. Uber Eats ~23%). Strength: dominant US restaurant density, DashPass membership, expanding into grocery and retail. Weakness: limited international presence, heavily US-dependent, not profitable at scale.
3. **Grab (SE Asia)** — Regional super-app in Southeast Asia (rides, delivery, payments). Strength: deep local integration, payments ecosystem. Weakness: limited to SE Asia, profitability challenges.
4. **Waymo / Cruise / Tesla (Autonomous Vehicles)** — Long-term structural threat/opportunity. Uber's strategy is to partner with AV companies (Waymo integration in Phoenix/SF) rather than build its own AV tech (sold ATG to Aurora in 2020).
5. **Public Transit / Micromobility** — Bikes, scooters, and transit are substitutes for short trips. Uber integrates transit and micromobility into the app as complements rather than competing directly.

## Our Differentiation

- Largest global mobility platform: 10,500+ cities across 70+ countries — network effects at global scale
- Multi-product platform: rides + delivery + freight in one app — no competitor matches this breadth
- Uber One membership (30M members) locks in cross-platform engagement and reduces churn by 50%
- Marketplace data advantage: billions of trips/deliveries train pricing, matching, and ETA algorithms
- AV-ready platform: partnerships with Waymo, Cruise, and others position Uber as the demand aggregator for autonomous fleets

## Competitive Threats

- **Autonomous vehicles** could disintermediate Uber's driver network if AV companies go direct-to-consumer (Waymo app). Uber's "AV platform" strategy mitigates but doesn't eliminate this risk.
- **DoorDash's US dominance** in delivery limits Uber Eats' growth in its largest market
- **Regulatory risk** remains the perennial concern — gig worker reclassification (EU, California), ride-hail licensing caps (NYC), and safety regulations
- **Super-apps in Asia** (Grab, Gojek, DiDi) limit Uber's ability to win in high-growth Asian markets
- **Inflation and cost of living** pressures reduce consumer demand for premium mobility and delivery services
