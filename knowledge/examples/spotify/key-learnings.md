# Key Learnings — Spotify

> Sources: Spotify earnings calls 2024, public investor communications

## Recent Experiment Results

- **Audiobook bundling (2024):** Including 15 hours/month of audiobooks in Premium drove incremental engagement and reduced churn in early markets. Helped differentiate vs. Apple Music and YouTube Music. Audible remains dominant but Spotify's bundled approach is a wedge.
- **Price increases (2023–2024):** Two consecutive years of price raises ($9.99→$10.99→$11.99 Individual in the US) showed lower-than-expected churn. Premium subscriber net adds remained strong at ~4M/quarter. Price elasticity was more favorable than modeled.
- **Podcast strategy pivot:** Shifting from expensive exclusives (spending ~$1B+ on Gimlet, Ringer, exclusive deals) to a licensed/open model significantly improved gross margins. Exclusive content did not drive enough incremental subscriber conversion to justify the cost.

## Focus Shifts

- **From growth-at-all-costs to profitability:** FY2024 was the first full-year operating profit. Layoffs in late 2023 (~17% workforce reduction) and podcast cost restructuring were the primary drivers.
- **From exclusive content to platform tools:** Investment shifted from buying exclusive podcasts to building creator/advertiser tools (SAX, Spotify for Artists enhancements).
- **From music-only to multi-format:** Audiobooks and video podcasts are now core to the strategy, not experiments.

## What We Know Now

- Free tier remains the most effective acquisition funnel — 39% of free users eventually convert to Premium
- Personalization is the #1 retention driver; users who engage with algorithmic playlists have 2x higher retention
- Ad revenue has significant headroom — Spotify's ad load and CPMs are well below social media and video platforms
- Creator tools (Marquee, Discovery Mode) are becoming a meaningful third revenue stream beyond subscriptions and ads
