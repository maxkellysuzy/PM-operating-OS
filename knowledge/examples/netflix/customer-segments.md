# Customer Segments — Netflix

> Sources: Netflix 2024 10-K, Q4 2024 Shareholder Letter, public disclosures

## Primary Segments

### Segment 1: Standard/Premium Subscribers (Ad-Free)
- **Who:** The core subscriber base across Standard ($15.49/mo US) and Premium ($22.99/mo US) plans. Households who value ad-free, high-quality streaming.
- **Needs:** Broad content library, original must-watch series and films, multiple simultaneous streams for family, 4K/HDR quality.
- **Pain points:** Rising prices (multiple increases 2022–2024), content they love getting cancelled, password sharing crackdown friction.

### Segment 2: Ad-Supported Tier Members
- **Who:** Price-sensitive viewers on the Standard with Ads plan ($6.99/mo US). 70M+ global MAUs by end of 2024. Growing fast in mature markets (US, UK, Brazil).
- **Needs:** Affordable access to Netflix content, acceptable ad experience (4–5 min/hour, lower than linear TV), most of the same catalog.
- **Pain points:** Some content missing due to licensing restrictions on ad tier, ad experience still maturing (relevance, frequency capping).

### Segment 3: International Markets (APAC, LATAM, EMEA)
- **Who:** Subscribers in 190+ countries. APAC and LATAM are growth markets with lower ARPU but large TAMs. ~60% of subscribers are outside UCAN.
- **Needs:** Local-language content (Korean, Indian, Spanish, Japanese originals), affordable pricing, mobile-friendly plans.
- **Pain points:** Lower willingness to pay vs. UCAN, competition from local streaming services (Hotstar, Viu, Globoplay), mobile-only viewing in many markets.

### Segment 4: Advertisers
- **Who:** Brands and agencies buying ads on the Netflix ad-supported tier.
- **Needs:** Premium brand-safe environment, reach cord-cutters and younger audiences, targeting capabilities, measurement.
- **Pain points:** Netflix's ad tech was initially third-party (Microsoft); in-house ad server only launched in 2024. Scale and targeting still catching up to YouTube and connected TV peers.

## Personas

- **Binge Watcher:** Watches 2+ hours/day, subscribes for original series (Squid Game, Wednesday, Stranger Things). High engagement, high retention.
- **Casual Household:** 2-4 profiles, watches a few times a week. Price-sensitive; ad tier or password sharing enforcement pushed some to downgrade.
- **International Mobile Viewer:** Watches primarily on phone in APAC/LATAM markets. Prefers local-language content. Lower ARPU but massive addressable market.
