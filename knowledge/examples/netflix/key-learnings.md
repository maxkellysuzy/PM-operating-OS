# Key Learnings — Netflix

> Sources: Netflix 2024 earnings calls, shareholder letters, public disclosures

## Recent Experiment Results

- **Paid sharing enforcement (2023–2024):** Crackdown on password sharing (100M+ households were using shared accounts) was the most consequential product change in years. Despite initial backlash, it drove the largest quarterly subscriber additions in Netflix history (13.1M in Q4 2023). Converted free-riders into paying members or ad-tier subscribers.
- **Ad tier launch (Nov 2022 → scaling 2024):** Grew from 0 to 70M+ MAUs in ~2 years. Key learning: price-sensitive customers who would have churned instead downgraded to the ad tier — it's a retention tool, not just an acquisition tool. CPMs are premium ($30–40 range) due to brand-safe, lean-back environment.
- **Live events (2024):** NFL Christmas Day games averaged 24.3M viewers (most-watched NFL Christmas games ever). Jake Paul vs. Mike Tyson drew 65M+ live viewers. Learning: live events drive massive cultural moments and new subscriber sign-ups, but operational complexity is significantly higher than on-demand.

## Focus Shifts

- **From subscriber growth to revenue growth:** Netflix stopped reporting quarterly subscribers in 2025, signaling the shift to revenue/profit as the primary narrative. ARM growth (via pricing and plan mix) matters more than raw sub count.
- **From licensed content to originals:** ~60%+ of viewing is now Netflix originals. Licensed content (Friends, The Office) was the initial catalog strategy, but originals drive differentiation and reduce supplier power.
- **From video-only to entertainment platform:** Games (70+ titles), live events, interactive content — expanding the definition of what Netflix is.

## What We Know Now

- Paid sharing enforcement had ~25% conversion rate — far better than feared. The key was giving users a clear, easy upgrade path.
- Ad-supported tier has high engagement (~35 hours/month per user) — comparable to ad-free tiers. Advertisers are willing to pay premium CPMs for the Netflix audience.
- International content travels globally — local-language content (Korean, Spanish, Japanese) is now a competitive advantage, not just a cost of doing business in local markets.
- Live programming creates engagement spikes but doesn't fundamentally change the daily viewing habit — it's additive, not a replacement for the core on-demand experience.
